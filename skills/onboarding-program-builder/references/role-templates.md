# Onboarding Templates by Role

Reusable onboarding programs for common roles.

---

## Software Engineer (IC2-IC3)

### First Week Focus
- **Technical:** Set up dev environment, understand architecture
- **Product:** Use the product, understand customer problems
- **People:** Meet team, establish buddy relationship
- **Ship:** Small bug fix or feature on Day 1-3

### Starter Projects (Good First Week)
- Fix "good first issue" bugs (labeled in GitHub)
- Add test coverage to existing features
- Improve documentation (README, architecture docs)
- Small feature additions (low risk, high learning)

### Key Relationships
- **Buddy:** Senior or Staff Engineer (pair programming, code review)
- **Manager:** Weekly 1:1s, unblock and support
- **PM:** Understand product roadmap and priorities
- **Designer:** Learn design system and collaboration process

### Learning Resources
- Architecture docs (system overview)
- Engineering handbook (best practices, tools, deploy process)
- Past RFCs (understand decision-making)
- Product strategy docs

### 30-Day Milestones
- ✅ Shipped 2-3 features or bug fixes
- ✅ Comfortable with dev environment and tools
- ✅ Participating in code reviews
- ✅ Understands team sprint/planning process

## Product Manager (IC2-IC3)

### First Week Focus
- **Product:** Deep dive on current product, roadmap, metrics
- **Customers:** Listen to customer calls, read support tickets
- **Team:** Meet engineering and design team
- **Ship:** Contribute to upcoming sprint planning

### Starter Projects
- Improve a spec or PRD
- Run customer interviews (5-10 interviews)
- Analyze a feature's metrics (what's working? what's not?)
- Sit in on sales demos (understand how we sell)

### Key Relationships
- **Buddy:** Senior PM (product sense, stakeholder management)
- **Manager:** Weekly 1:1s, coaching on PM craft
- **Engineering Lead:** Understand technical constraints and process
- **Designer:** Collaboration on discovery and specs
- **Customer Success:** Hear customer feedback firsthand

### Learning Resources
- Product strategy docs
- User personas and research
- Metrics dashboards (understand what we measure)
- Competitive analysis
- Past product launches (post-mortems)

### 30-Day Milestones
- ✅ Led customer discovery (interviews, surveys)
- ✅ Shipped a spec or feature
- ✅ Understands product metrics deeply
- ✅ Building trust with eng and design

## Account Executive (Sales)

### First Week Focus
- **Product:** Become product expert (demos, use cases, positioning)
- **Sales Process:** Understand sales methodology, CRM, playbooks
- **Customers:** Listen to calls, read win/loss notes
- **Pipeline:** Shadow senior AEs on discovery and demos

### Starter Activities
- **Week 1:** Product training (can demo core product)
- **Week 2:** Shadow 10+ sales calls (discovery, demo, close)
- **Week 3:** Lead demo with support
- **Week 4:** Take over inbound leads, start outbound prospecting

### Key Relationships
- **Buddy:** Top-performing AE (learn from the best)
- **Manager:** Weekly 1:1s, deal coaching, forecast reviews
- **SDR Team:** Pipeline generation partnership
- **CS Team:** Learn post-sale process, renewals
- **Sales Eng:** Technical demo support

### Learning Resources
- Sales playbook (pitch deck, objection handling, case studies)
- Competitive battle cards
- Pricing and packaging docs
- Customer case studies (win stories)
- Win/loss analysis (why we win, why we lose)

### 30-Day Milestones (Ramp is slower)
- ✅ Can deliver product demo independently
- ✅ Taking inbound leads (with manager shadow)
- ✅ Understands sales process and tools
- ✅ First deal in pipeline (even if small)

### 90-Day Goal
- Close first deal
- 50% of quota attainment
- Healthy pipeline (3x quota coverage)

## Customer Success Manager

### First Week Focus
- **Product:** Become expert user (know it better than customers)
- **Customers:** Shadow CSM calls, read customer health scores
- **Processes:** Understand onboarding, renewals, expansion process
- **Tools:** Learn CSM platform (Gainsight, ChurnZero, etc.)

### Starter Activities
- **Week 1:** Product deep-dive, shadow 10+ customer calls
- **Week 2:** Take on 5-10 smaller accounts (with buddy support)
- **Week 3:** Lead customer onboarding call
- **Week 4:** Run business review

### Key Relationships
- **Buddy:** Senior CSM (customer relationship skills)
- **Manager:** Weekly 1:1s, account strategy
- **Sales Team:** Handoff process, account context
- **Product Team:** Feature requests, customer feedback loop
- **Support Team:** Escalation process

### Learning Resources
- Customer onboarding playbook
- Health score methodology
- Renewal and expansion playbooks
- Customer success metrics (NRR, GRR, CSAT)
- Top customer case studies

### 30-Day Milestones
- ✅ Taking on book of business (10-15 accounts)
- ✅ Can run customer onboarding
- ✅ Understands health scores and renewal process
- ✅ Building customer relationships

## Engineering Manager

### First Week Focus
- **Team:** Meet every team member (1:1s)
- **Codebase:** Understand what team owns
- **Process:** Learn sprint process, release cadence, on-call
- **Stakeholders:** Meet PM, Designer, cross-functional partners

### Starter Activities
- **Week 1:** 1:1s with all reports (get to know them)
- **Week 2:** Attend all team ceremonies (observe, don't change yet)
- **Week 3:** Run sprint planning or retro
- **Week 4:** Start making small process improvements

### Key Relationships
- **Buddy:** Another EM or Director (management coaching)
- **Manager:** Weekly 1:1s, leadership coaching
- **Direct Reports:** Weekly 1:1s with each (build trust)
- **Product Manager:** Daily collaboration
- **Other EMs:** Peer support, share learnings

### Learning Resources
- Team docs (what we own, how we work)
- Past performance reviews (understand team members)
- Engineering handbook (processes, tools, standards)
- 1:1 templates and coaching guides
- Manager training materials

### 30-Day Milestones
- ✅ Built trust with team (they feel heard)
- ✅ Understands team dynamics and individuals
- ✅ Running team ceremonies effectively
- ✅ Partnering with PM on roadmap

## Design (Product Designer)

### First Week Focus
- **Product:** Use product extensively, understand UX
- **Design System:** Learn components, patterns, guidelines
- **Team:** Meet PM, eng team, other designers
- **Process:** Understand design review, feedback, handoff process

### Starter Projects
- Improve existing UI (polish project)
- Contribute to design system
- Create mockups for upcoming feature
- User research (interviews or usability testing)

### Key Relationships
- **Buddy:** Senior Designer (design craft, feedback)
- **Manager:** Weekly 1:1s, portfolio reviews
- **PM:** Daily collaboration on product decisions
- **Engineers:** Design handoff and implementation partnership
- **User Researcher:** (if separate role) Learn research process

### Learning Resources
- Design system documentation
- Brand guidelines
- User research findings
- Product strategy and roadmap
- Past design work (case studies, Figma files)

### 30-Day Milestones
- ✅ Shipped a design (implemented by eng)
- ✅ Contributing to design system
- ✅ Participating in design reviews
- ✅ Comfortable with tools and process

## Common Elements Across All Roles

### Universal Day 1
- Welcome by manager (personal connection)
- IT setup (get systems working)
- Company orientation (mission, values, culture)
- Team lunch (meet everyone)
- First small task (ship something)
- Day 1 check-in (how was it?)

### Universal Week 1
- Daily check-ins with buddy
- Meet key people (team, cross-functional)
- Learn tools and systems
- Starter project (low risk, high learning)
- Friday demo or retro (share learnings)

### Universal Check-Ins
- **30 Days:** How's it going? What's working? What's not?
- **60 Days:** Feeling ramped? What's your biggest win?
- **90 Days:** Formal review, career conversation

### Universal Buddy System
- Peer-level buddy
- Daily check-ins Week 1
- Weekly check-ins Month 1
- Bi-weekly check-ins Months 2-3
- Ongoing friendship/mentorship

### Universal Success Metrics
- Time to first commit/contribution
- 30-day satisfaction score
- 90-day performance rating
- 1-year retention

## Customization by Level

### Junior (IC1-IC2)
- More structured learning
- Smaller starter projects
- Daily check-ins (not just weekly)
- Explicit skill-building goals
- Frequent feedback

### Mid-Level (IC3, M3)
- Moderate structure
- Balance learning and contributing
- Weekly check-ins
- Autonomy increases quickly
- Mentorship opportunities (help juniors)

### Senior (IC4+, M4+)
- Less structure, more autonomy
- Expect them to ramp faster
- Strategic projects sooner
- Leadership expectations (influence, mentor)
- Bi-weekly check-ins

## Using These Templates

**For Individual Plans:**
- Start with role template
- Customize for specific person (their background, team, projects)
- Add names (manager, buddy, team members)
- Set specific dates and milestones

**For Scalable Programs:**
- Use template as-is for all new hires in that role
- Update quarterly based on feedback
- Maintain in wiki/Notion for easy access
- Train managers and buddies on program
