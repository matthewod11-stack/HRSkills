## Example 2: Morgan Taylor - Enterprise AE (IC3)

**Profile:**
- **Role:** Enterprise Account Executive
- **Start Date:** February 12, 2024
- **Department:** Sales
- **Manager:** VP Sales
- **Buddy:** Top performing AE (Jamie)
- **Background:** 5 years sales experience, coming from competitor

---

### Pre-Boarding (Week of Feb 5)

**Welcome Email:**
```
From: VP Sales
To: Morgan Taylor
Subject: Welcome to the Sales Team!

Morgan,

Excited to have you joining us! You're going to crush it here.

Your first two weeks will be intensive product training. By end of Week 2, you'll be able to run a full demo solo. Week 3, you'll start taking inbound leads. Week 4, you'll get your first accounts assigned.

**What to expect:**
- Week 1: Product boot camp (become product expert)
- Week 2: Shadow sales calls (learn our methodology)
- Week 3: Run demos with support
- Week 4: Own deals

Your laptop and sales kit will arrive by Feb 8. Jamie (your buddy, our #1 rep) will reach out this week to introduce himself.

Let's go close some deals!

[VP Sales]
```

---

### Week 1: Product Boot Camp (Feb 12-16)

**Monday-Wednesday: Product Training**
- 8 hours of product deep-dive
- Use cases, positioning, competitive differentiation
- Demo environment setup
- Practice demos (to PM, to Sales Eng, to manager)

**Thursday-Friday: Shadow Calls**
- Observe 10+ sales calls
- Discovery, demo, objection handling, closing
- Take notes, ask questions afterward

**Week 1 Goal:** Can deliver core product demo confidently

---

### Week 2: Sales Process (Feb 19-23)

**Monday-Tuesday: Methodology Training**
- Sales playbook walkthrough
- CRM training (Salesforce)
- Forecasting and pipeline management
- Comp plan and quota explanation

**Wednesday-Friday: Shadow More Calls**
- Shadow Jamie on 5 deals (different stages)
- Shadow VP Sales on enterprise deal
- Start to lead demo (with support)

**Week 2 Goal:** Understand full sales cycle and methodology

---

### Weeks 3-4: Ramp to Owning Deals

**Week 3:**
- Take inbound leads (with Jamie shadowing)
- Run discovery calls
- Deliver demos
- Manager reviews every call (coaching)

**Week 4:**
- Own full deals (smaller accounts to start)

## Using These Examples

**For Creating Individual Plans:**
1. Start with role template (Engineer, Sales, etc.)
2. Customize for the person (their experience, team, manager)
3. Add specific names, dates, projects
4. Set clear milestones and check-ins

**For Creating Programs:**
1. Generalize the plan (remove names, specific dates)
2. Make it repeatable (templates, checklists)
3. Train managers and buddies
4. Iterate based on feedback
