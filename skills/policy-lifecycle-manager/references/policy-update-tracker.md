## Policy Rollout Checklist

Use this checklist every time you create or update a major policy.

### Phase 1: Policy Creation
- [ ] Identify need (legal requirement, business need, risk mitigation)
- [ ] Research relevant laws (federal, state, industry)
- [ ] Draft policy (use templates from `policy-library.md`)
- [ ] Review with stakeholders (leadership, affected departments)
- [ ] Legal review (employment attorney)
- [ ] Final approval (CEO, board if applicable)

### Phase 2: Communication Planning
- [ ] Determine effective date (at least 30 days notice for major changes)
- [ ] Create communication plan:
  - [ ] All-hands announcement
  - [ ] Email to all employees
  - [ ] Manager talking points
  - [ ] FAQ document
- [ ] Plan training (if needed):
  - [ ] Manager training (how to enforce policy)
  - [ ] Employee training (especially for compliance policies like anti-harassment)

### Phase 3: Rollout
- [ ] Update employee handbook
- [ ] Post updated handbook to intranet/shared drive
- [ ] Announce policy:
  - [ ] CEO/leadership email
  - [ ] All-hands presentation
  - [ ] Slack/Teams announcement
- [ ] Distribute acknowledgment form (electronic or paper)
- [ ] Track acknowledgments (see tracker above)
- [ ] Send reminders to employees who haven't acknowledged

### Phase 4: Post-Rollout
- [ ] 100% acknowledgment achieved
- [ ] Archive old policy version
- [ ] Update policy tracker spreadsheet
- [ ] Schedule annual review date
- [ ] Conduct training (if required)
- [ ] Monitor compliance (spot-check enforcement, collect feedback)

## Policy Review Calendar

Schedule policy reviews to ensure you stay compliant and up-to-date.

### Annual Review (Minimum)
Review all policies at least once per year, even if no changes are needed.

**Recommended Schedule:**
- **Q1 (January-March):** Review compensation, benefits, and time off policies (before annual enrollment, raises)
- **Q2 (April-June):** Review workplace conduct, code of ethics, anti-harassment (refresh training)
- **Q3 (July-September):** Review performance management, discipline, and separation policies
- **Q4 (October-December):** Review employment basics, compliance policies, prepare for new year

---

### Trigger Events (Review Immediately)
Review and update policies when:
- **New law or regulation** (e.g., new state sick leave law, EEOC guidance)
- **Court ruling** impacts your industry or state
- **Business change** (e.g., going remote, opening new office, new benefits)
- **Employee relations issue** reveals policy gap (e.g., harassment complaint highlights weak reporting process)
- **Acquisition or merger** (align policies across companies)

## Policy Compliance Monitoring

After rolling out a policy, monitor compliance:

### Metrics to Track
- **Acknowledgment Rate:** % of employees who've acknowledged the policy
- **Training Completion:** % of employees who've completed required training (e.g., harassment prevention)
- **Policy Violations:** Number of reported violations (track by policy, department, severity)
- **Time to Resolution:** Average time to resolve policy violations or complaints

### Monthly/Quarterly Review
- [ ] Review acknowledgment tracker: Follow up with employees who haven't signed
- [ ] Review training completion: Send reminders for overdue training
- [ ] Review violations: Identify patterns (e.g., one department has frequent attendance issues)
- [ ] Manager feedback: Are policies clear? Are they enforceable?

## Policy Sunset / Archive Process

When a policy is no longer needed:

1. **Mark as "Archived"** in policy tracker
2. **Communicate to employees** (e.g., "We're replacing our old Travel Policy with a simpler Expense Reimbursement policy")
3. **Remove from active handbook** but keep archived copy
4. **Retain archived policy** for legal purposes (7+ years recommended)

**Why archive instead of delete?**
- Legal defense: If an employee sues for something that happened under the old policy, you need to prove what the policy was at the time
- Audit trail: Track the evolution of your policies over time
