# CATEGORY 1: EMPLOYMENT BASICS

## Policy 1.1: Equal Employment Opportunity (EEO)

**Purpose:** Ensure compliance with federal and state anti-discrimination laws

**Policy:**

[Company Name] is an equal opportunity employer. We do not discriminate based on race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, genetic information, or any other characteristic protected by federal, state, or local law.

This policy applies to all employment decisions, including:
- Recruiting and hiring
- Promotions and transfers
- Compensation and benefits
- Training and development
- Discipline and termination

Any employee who believes they have experienced discrimination should report it immediately to HR at [email/phone]. The company prohibits retaliation against anyone who reports discrimination in good faith.

## Policy 1.2: At-Will Employment

**Purpose:** Clarify the at-will nature of employment (CRITICAL for legal protection)

**Policy:**

Employment with [Company Name] is at-will. This means that either you or the company may terminate the employment relationship at any time, with or without cause, and with or without notice.

Nothing in this handbook, your offer letter, or any other company document creates an employment contract, express or implied. No manager, supervisor, or employee of the company has the authority to alter the at-will relationship, except the [CEO/authorized officer] in a written agreement signed by the [CEO/authorized officer].

This at-will relationship cannot be modified except in a written document signed by [CEO/authorized officer].

**Implementation Notes:**
- Place this prominently (first page of handbook)
- Include in offer letters
- Require written acknowledgment

## Policy 1.4: Background Checks

**Purpose:** Ensure safe hiring practices

**Policy:**

[Company Name] may conduct background checks on candidates as part of the hiring process. Background checks may include:
- Criminal history
- Employment verification
- Education verification
- Credit history (for finance/sensitive roles only)
- Motor vehicle records (for driving roles only)

We comply with the Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA) and state law. You'll receive:
- A disclosure that we may conduct a background check
- Your written authorization
- A copy of the report (if we take adverse action based on it)

A criminal conviction does not automatically disqualify you. We consider the nature of the offense, time elapsed, and relevance to the job.

**State-Specific Note:**
- **California, New York:** Ban-the-box laws apply. We don't ask about criminal history until after a conditional offer.

## Policy 2.3: Family and Medical Leave (FMLA)

**Purpose:** Comply with FMLA (required for employers with 50+ employees)

**Policy:**

Eligible employees may take up to 12 weeks of unpaid leave in a 12-month period for:
- Birth or adoption of a child
- Serious health condition (employee's own)
- Serious health condition of a family member (spouse, child, parent)
- Military family leave (care for injured service member or qualifying exigency)

**Eligibility:**
- Worked for the company for at least 12 months
- Worked at least 1,250 hours in the past 12 months
- Work at a location with 50+ employees within 75 miles

**Job Protection:** Your position (or an equivalent position) will be held for you during FMLA leave.

**Benefits:** Health insurance continues during FMLA leave (you pay your normal premium share).

**How to Request:** Contact HR at least 30 days in advance (if foreseeable). HR will provide required forms and certification.

**Certification:** Medical certification may be required.

**State Expansions:**
- **California (CFRA):** Expands to all employers with 5+ employees, includes grandparents/siblings/grandchildren
- **New York (Paid Family Leave):** Up to 12 weeks paid leave (funded by payroll tax)

2. Report it immediately to your manager, HR [email], or [CEO]
3. You may report anonymously via [hotline/form]

**Investigation:** All complaints will be investigated promptly and confidentially.

**Non-Retaliation:** We prohibit retaliation against anyone who reports harassment in good faith or participates in an investigation.

**Consequences:** Employees who engage in harassment will face disciplinary action, up to and including termination.

**Mandatory Training (if applicable):**
- **California:** All employees must complete sexual harassment training [within 6 months (supervisors) / 1 year (employees), then every 2 years]

# CATEGORY 5: TECHNOLOGY & SECURITY

## Policy 5.1: Acceptable Use of Technology

**Policy:**

Company-provided technology (computers, phones, email, Slack, etc.) is for business use. Personal use is allowed but should be minimal.

**Prohibited Uses:**
- Illegal activities
- Accessing, storing, or sharing offensive, obscene, or discriminatory content
- Installing unauthorized software or apps
- Downloading pirated content
- Using company resources for personal business ventures

**Monitoring:** The company reserves the right to monitor email, internet usage, and other company systems. Do not expect privacy when using company technology.

**Violations may result in disciplinary action.**

---

## Policy 5.2: Data Security and Privacy

**Policy:**

Employees must protect company and customer data from unauthorized access, loss, or theft.

**Security Requirements:**
- Use strong passwords (12+ characters, unique for each system)
- Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on all accounts
- Do not share passwords
- Lock your computer when you step away
- Encrypt sensitive data
- Report lost or stolen devices immediately

**Phishing and Security Incidents:**
- Do not click suspicious links or download unexpected attachments
- Report phishing attempts and security incidents to IT immediately at [email]

**Violations may result in disciplinary action.**

---

## Policy 5.3: Bring Your Own Device (BYOD)

**Policy:**

Employees may use personal devices (phones, tablets, laptops) for work, subject to the following conditions:

**Requirements:**
- Install required security software (e.g., MDM, encryption)
- Use strong passwords and enable 2FA
- Do not store sensitive company data on personal devices (use cloud storage instead)
- Allow IT to remotely wipe company data if the device is lost or stolen

**If you leave the company, you must delete all company data from your personal device.**

## Policy 6.3: Progressive Discipline

**Policy:**

[Company Name] generally follows a progressive discipline approach when performance or conduct issues arise:

1. **Verbal Coaching:** Manager provides feedback and coaching
2. **Written Warning:** Documented warning placed in employee file
3. **Performance Improvement Plan (PIP):** 30-90 day plan with specific goals and check-ins
4. **Suspension or Termination**

**However, the company reserves the right to skip steps or proceed directly to termination for:**
- Serious misconduct (harassment, violence, theft, fraud)
- Gross negligence or willful violation of policy
- Repeated violations despite prior warnings

**Progressive discipline is a guideline, not a guarantee.** Employment remains at-will.

---

## Policy 6.4: Performance Improvement Plans (PIPs)

**Policy:**

A Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) is a structured process to help employees improve performance or behavior.

**When PIPs Are Used:**
- Failure to meet job expectations
- Performance decline
- Behavioral issues (unprofessionalism, attendance, etc.)

**PIP Components:**
- Specific performance gaps or issues
- Measurable goals and success criteria
- Timeline (typically 30, 60, or 90 days)
- Manager support and check-ins (weekly or bi-weekly)

**Possible Outcomes:**
- **Successfully Complete PIP:** Return to good standing
- **Fail to Meet Goals:** Termination or extension (at company discretion)

**A PIP is not a guarantee of continued employment.** Employment remains at-will.

## Policy 7.2: Termination

**Policy:**

[Company Name] may terminate your employment at any time, with or without cause, consistent with at-will employment.

**Reasons for Termination May Include:**
- Performance issues (failure to meet expectations, despite coaching/PIP)
- Misconduct (harassment, theft, violence, policy violations)
- Elimination of position (layoff, restructuring)
- Business needs

**You will receive:**
- Final paycheck (see state law requirements below)
- COBRA continuation coverage notice (if eligible)
- Information about benefits (unused PTO payout, 401k rollover, etc.)

**Final Paycheck:**
- **California:** Due immediately upon termination
- **Other states:** Varies (next regular payday or within X days)
