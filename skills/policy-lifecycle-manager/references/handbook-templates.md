## **1. Employment Basics**

### **1.1 At-Will Employment**

**Employment with [Company Name] is at-will.** This means either you or the company can end the employment relationship at any time, with or without cause, and with or without notice.

Nothing in this handbook, your offer letter, or any other company document creates an employment contract. No manager or employee can alter this at-will relationship except the CEO in a signed written agreement.

---

### **1.2 Equal Employment Opportunity**

[Company Name] is an equal opportunity employer. We don't discriminate based on:
- Race, color, national origin, ancestry
- Religion or creed
- Sex, gender, gender identity, gender expression
- Sexual orientation
- Age (40 and over)
- Disability (physical or mental)
- Genetic information
- Pregnancy, childbirth, or related medical conditions
- Military or veteran status
- Marital status
- Any other characteristic protected by law

This policy applies to all employment decisions, including hiring, promotions, compensation, training, discipline, and termination.

If you believe you've experienced discrimination, report it to HR immediately. We prohibit retaliation against anyone who reports discrimination in good faith.

---

### **1.3 Immigration Compliance**

All employees must complete Form I-9 and provide proof of identity and authorization to work in the U.S. within three business days of their start date.

## **4. Code of Conduct**

### **4.1 Professionalism**

We expect everyone to be respectful, collaborative, and professional. This includes:
- Treating colleagues, customers, and partners with respect
- Communicating clearly and constructively
- Meeting deadlines and commitments
- Taking ownership of mistakes

---

### **4.2 Confidentiality**

You may have access to confidential information (customer data, financials, product roadmaps, etc.). You must:
- Keep confidential information secure
- Not share it outside the company without authorization
- Not use it for personal gain

**This obligation continues after you leave the company.**

---

### **4.3 Conflicts of Interest**

Avoid situations where your personal interests conflict with the company's interests.

**Examples:**
- Working for a competitor
- Accepting gifts or payments from vendors
- Hiring a family member without disclosure

**If you think you have a conflict of interest, disclose it to your manager or HR.**

---

### **4.4 Social Media**

You're free to use social media, but:
- Don't post confidential company information
- Don't speak on behalf of the company unless authorized
- Don't post anything that could harm the company's reputation
- Be respectful (avoid harassment, discrimination, etc.)

**Personal accounts:** Make it clear you're speaking for yourself, not the company.

---

## **5. Anti-Harassment and Discrimination**

### **5.1 Policy**

[Company Name] prohibits harassment and discrimination based on any protected characteristic (see Section 1.2).

**Harassment includes:**
- Unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, or sexual comments
- Offensive jokes, slurs, or epithets based on protected characteristics
- Intimidation, threats, or bullying
- Displaying offensive images or materials

**Sexual harassment includes:**
- Quid pro quo (e.g., "go on a date with me or you're fired")
- Hostile work environment (pervasive unwelcome sexual conduct)

---

### **5.2 Reporting**

If you experience or witness harassment or discrimination:

1. **Tell the person to stop** (if you feel safe doing so)
2. **Report it immediately** to:
   - Your manager
   - HR at [hr@company.com] or [phone]
   - [Company Name]'s CEO at [email]

**You can report anonymously** via [anonymous hotline or form].

---

### **5.3 Investigation**

We'll investigate all complaints promptly and confidentially. Investigations may include:
- Interviewing the complainant, accused, and witnesses
- Reviewing documents, emails, or Slack messages
- Taking interim measures (e.g., separation of parties)

**We prohibit retaliation** against anyone who reports harassment in good faith or participates in an investigation.

---

### **5.4 Consequences**

Employees who engage in harassment or discrimination will face disciplinary action, up to and including termination.

---

## **6. Workplace Safety**

### **6.1 Health and Safety**

We're committed to providing a safe workplace. You must:
- Follow all safety rules and procedures
- Report hazards or unsafe conditions immediately
- Use required safety equipment

---

### **6.2 Workers' Compensation**

If you're injured at work:
1. **Seek medical attention** if needed
2. **Report the injury to your manager and HR within 24 hours**
3. HR will provide workers' compensation claim forms

---

### **6.3 Drug-Free Workplace**

[Company Name] prohibits the use, possession, or distribution of illegal drugs or alcohol on company property or during work hours.

**Exceptions:** Alcohol may be served at company-sponsored events. Prescription medications are allowed if used as prescribed.

**Violations may result in termination.**

---

## **7. Technology and Data Security**

### **7.1 Company Equipment**

Company-provided equipment (laptops, phones, etc.) is for business use. Personal use is allowed but should be minimal.

**You must:**
- Protect equipment from damage, loss, or theft
- Return all equipment upon termination
- Not install unauthorized software

---

### **7.2 Data Security**

You must:
- Use strong passwords and enable two-factor authentication
- Not share passwords
- Encrypt sensitive data
- Report security incidents (phishing, data breaches) to IT immediately

---

### **7.3 Email and Communication Monitoring**

The company reserves the right to monitor email, Slack, and other company communication tools. Do not use company systems for illegal or inappropriate purposes.

---

## **8. Performance and Discipline**

### **8.1 Performance Reviews**

We conduct formal performance reviews [annually / semi-annually]. Your manager will also provide ongoing feedback.

---

### **8.2 Discipline**

We generally follow progressive discipline:
1. Verbal coaching
2. Written warning
3. Performance improvement plan (PIP)
4. Suspension or termination

**However, we reserve the right to skip steps or proceed directly to termination for serious misconduct** (theft, fraud, harassment, violence, etc.).

---

## **9. Separation**

### **9.1 Resignation**

If you resign, provide at least [2 weeks] notice in writing.

---

### **9.2 Termination**

The company may terminate your employment at any time, with or without cause (at-will employment).

---

### **9.3 Final Paycheck**

**California:** Due immediately upon termination (if terminated) or within 72 hours (if you resign without notice).

**Other states:** Varies. HR will provide details.

---

### **9.4 Return of Property**

You must return all company property (laptop, badge, documents) on your last day.

## State-Specific Addendums

For multi-state companies, include addendums for each state with unique requirements.

### **California Addendum**

**Meal and Rest Breaks:**
- 30-minute unpaid meal break for shifts over 5 hours (must start before end of 5th hour)
- Second meal break for shifts over 10 hours
- 10-minute paid rest break for every 4 hours worked (or major fraction thereof)

**Sick Leave:**
- Minimum 24 hours (3 days) per year
- Accrues at 1 hour per 30 hours worked
- Carryover required (up to 48 hours cap)

**Final Pay:**
- Termination: Due immediately
- Resignation with 72+ hours notice: Due on last day
- Resignation without notice: Due within 72 hours

**Sexual Harassment Training:**
- Supervisors: 2 hours every 2 years
- Employees: 1 hour every 2 years
- Must be provided within 6 months of hire (supervisors) or 1 year (employees)

**Lactation Accommodation:**
- Private space (not a bathroom) for expressing breast milk
- Reasonable break time

**Wage Theft Protection Act:**
- Provide written notice of pay rate, pay schedule, and employer info at hire

---

### **New York Addendum**

**Sick Leave:**
- Varies by employer size (5-14 employees: 40 hours unpaid; 15+ employees: 40 hours paid)

**Sexual Harassment Prevention:**
- Annual training required for all employees
- Must provide complaint form and distribute policy annually

**Wage Notice:**
- Provide written notice of pay rate, pay day, employer info at hire and upon any change

---

### **Washington Addendum**

**Paid Family and Medical Leave:**
- Up to 12 weeks for family/medical reasons, 16 weeks for pregnancy
- Funded by payroll tax (shared employer/employee)

**Sick Leave:**
- 1 hour per 40 hours worked
- Can be used for safe leave (domestic violence, stalking, sexual assault)
