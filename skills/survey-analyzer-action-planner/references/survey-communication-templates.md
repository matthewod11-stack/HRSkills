# Survey Communication Templates

Complete templates for announcing survey results, sharing action plans, and providing progress updates.

---

## Overview: Communication Principles

**The golden rule:** If you ask for feedback, you must close the loop.

**Communication flow:**
```
Pre-Survey (Build Excitement) ‚Üí During Survey (Drive Participation) ‚Üí Results Announcement (Transparency) ‚Üí Action Plan (Commitment) ‚Üí Progress Updates (Accountability) ‚Üí Next Survey (Show Impact)
```

**Why communication matters:**
- ‚úÖ **Good communication:** Builds trust, drives engagement, increases next year's response rate
- ‚ùå **Bad communication:** "We asked, nothing changed" ‚Üí Survey response rate tanks ‚Üí You lose valuable feedback channel

## Communication Best Practices

### 1. Transparency

**Do:**
- ‚úÖ Share results company-wide (overall + department-level)
- ‚úÖ Acknowledge bad news ("Career development scored low. We need to fix this.")
- ‚úÖ Show data (charts, heat maps, trends)

**Don't:**
- ‚ùå Hide results ("Survey results are confidential, leadership only")
- ‚ùå Spin bad news ("Our engagement is only 50%, but that's great for our industry!") ‚Üê dishonest
- ‚ùå Cherry-pick data ("Look at our high scores, ignore the low ones")

---

### 2. Speed

**Timeline:**
- Survey closes ‚Üí Results shared within 3-4 weeks (no more than 1 month)
- Why? Employees lose interest if you wait too long

**Bad example:**
- Survey closes in November ‚Üí "We'll share results in Q1" (3+ months later) ‚ùå

**Good example:**
- Survey closes Nov 15 ‚Üí Results shared at Dec 10 all-hands (3.5 weeks) ‚úÖ

---

### 3. Action-Oriented

**Every communication must include:**
1. **What we heard** (survey results)
2. **What we're doing** (action plan)
3. **How we'll track progress** (milestones, timeline)

**Bad example:**
- "Thank you for completing the survey. We'll review and get back to you." ‚ùå (vague, no action)

**Good example:**
- "Career development scored 45%. We're creating career ladders (by Q2), training managers (by Q1), and launching Lunch & Learn (by Q2). We'll measure progress in Q3 pulse survey." ‚úÖ (specific, actionable)

---

### 4. Consistency

**Commit to a communication cadence:**
- **Monthly:** Progress update at all-hands (5 min)
- **Quarterly:** Email update + pulse survey
- **Annually:** Full engagement survey + results presentation

**Why consistency matters:** Employees need to see sustained commitment (not just one-time reaction)

---

### 5. Two-Way Communication

**Don't just broadcast results ‚Äî create dialogue:**

- **All-hands Q&A:** Let employees ask questions about results and action plan
- **Manager discussions:** Managers discuss team results in team meetings
- **Open office hours:** Leadership holds office hours for survey follow-up questions
- **Slack channel:** #survey-questions for ongoing discussion

## Common Communication Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Results without action plan

**What it looks like:**
- Share survey results ‚Üí "Thank you for your feedback!" ‚Üí No follow-up

**Why it fails:**
- Employees feel unheard ("They asked, but nothing changed")
- Next year's response rate tanks

**Fix:**
- Always pair results with action plan (even if plan is simple)

---

### Mistake 2: Vague commitments

**What it looks like:**
- "We'll work on career development" (no specifics)
- "We hear you on workload" (no action)

**Why it fails:**
- No accountability (who's doing what by when?)

**Fix:**
- SMART actions: "Create career ladders by Q2 (Owner: VP People)"

---

### Mistake 3: Over-promising

**What it looks like:**
- "We'll fix everything in the next 3 months!"

**Why it fails:**
- Unrealistic ‚Üí Can't deliver ‚Üí Lose trust

**Fix:**
- Be honest about what's achievable: "Career development will take 12 months to fully address. Here are our Q1, Q2, Q3 milestones."

---

### Mistake 4: Radio silence after launch

**What it looks like:**
- Share action plan in November ‚Üí No updates for 6 months

**Why it fails:**
- Employees assume you forgot / gave up

**Fix:**
- Monthly updates (even if small: "We're on track. Career ladders 40% done.")

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Close the loop** - If you ask for feedback, you must communicate what you're doing with it
2. **Be transparent** - Share good and bad news (hiding results destroys trust)
3. **Be fast** - Communicate results within 3-4 weeks of survey close
4. **Pair results with action** - Never share results without action plan
5. **Show progress** - Monthly updates, quarterly pulse surveys
6. **Be honest** - Don't over-promise, acknowledge challenges
7. **Create dialogue** - Two-way communication (Q&A, office hours, Slack)

**Great communication = transparency + speed + action + consistency.**
