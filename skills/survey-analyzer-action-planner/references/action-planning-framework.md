# Action Planning Framework

Complete guide to translating survey insights into concrete action plans that drive organizational change.

---

## Overview: From Insights to Action

**The bridge:**
```
Survey Results ‚Üí Prioritization ‚Üí Root Cause Analysis ‚Üí SMART Actions ‚Üí Ownership ‚Üí Implementation ‚Üí Measurement
```

**Common failure mode:**
- ‚ùå Share survey results ‚Üí "Thanks for feedback!" ‚Üí Nothing changes ‚Üí Next year's survey response rate tanks

**Success model:**
- ‚úÖ Share results ‚Üí Prioritized action plan ‚Üí Clear owners ‚Üí Execute ‚Üí Measure impact ‚Üí Communicate progress

## Step 1: Prioritization

**You can't fix everything. Prioritize ruthlessly.**

---

### The 2x2 Prioritization Matrix

**Axes:**
- **X-axis:** Impact on engagement (Low ‚Üí High)
- **Y-axis:** Current score (High ‚Üí Low)

**Quadrants:**

|                     | Low Impact | High Impact |
|---------------------|------------|-------------|
| **Low Score (Problem)** | üü° Nice to have | üî¥ **PRIORITY 1** |
| **High Score (Strength)** | ‚ö™ Maintain | üü¢ Protect |

---

**üî¥ Priority 1: High Impact + Low Score**
- **Action:** Fix these first (biggest ROI)
- **Example:** Career development (45% favorable, 0.75 correlation to eNPS)

**üü¢ Protect: High Impact + High Score**
- **Action:** Don't break what's working (keep doing it)
- **Example:** Manager effectiveness (85% favorable, 0.72 correlation to eNPS)

**üü° Nice to have: Low Impact + Low Score**
- **Action:** Deprioritize (won't move engagement)
- **Example:** Office comfort (50% favorable, 0.20 correlation to eNPS)

**‚ö™ Maintain: Low Impact + High Score**
- **Action:** No change needed
- **Example:** Free snacks (80% favorable, 0.10 correlation to eNPS)

---

### The 3-5 Rule

**Pick 3-5 top priorities. No more.**

**Why?**
- **Trying to fix 10 things = fixing nothing** (diluted resources, no accountability)
- **Focus on 3-5 = real progress** (concentrated effort, clear ownership)

**Exception:** If you have segmented issues (e.g., Operations department crisis), create department-specific action plan.

## Step 2: Root Cause Analysis

**Don't treat symptoms. Find root causes.**

---

### The 5 Whys Technique

**Example 1: Career Development**

**Problem:** "I have opportunities for career growth" = 45% favorable

**5 Whys:**
1. **Why is career growth scored low?**
   - "Employees don't see promotion opportunities"

2. **Why don't they see promotion opportunities?**
   - "No clear criteria for promotion (what does it take to get promoted?)"

3. **Why don't we have clear promotion criteria?**
   - "We never documented career ladders"

4. **Why haven't we documented career ladders?**
   - "Company grew fast (30 ‚Üí 250 employees in 2 years), systems didn't keep up"

5. **Why didn't systems keep up?**
   - "HR is under-resourced (1 HRBP for 250 employees)"

**Root cause:** HR capacity issue + lack of documented career frameworks

**Solutions:**
- **Short-term:** Train managers to have career conversations with their teams (even without formal ladders)
- **Medium-term:** Hire HR to build career ladders (or contract with external consultant)
- **Long-term:** Build career development into performance review process

---

**Example 2: Manager Effectiveness (Specific Team)**

**Problem:** Linda's team (Operations) scored 30% on "My manager is effective"

**5 Whys:**
1. **Why is Linda's team unhappy with her management?**
   - "Employees say she doesn't provide clear direction"

2. **Why doesn't she provide clear direction?**
   - "She's new to management (promoted 6 months ago from IC)"

3. **Why was she promoted without management training?**
   - "We promote based on technical skill, not management readiness"

4. **Why do we promote based on technical skill?**
   - "No formal criteria for what makes a good manager"

5. **Why don't we have criteria for good managers?**
   - "Company hasn't defined leadership competencies"

**Root cause:** Linda is undertrained + company promotes ICs without preparing them for management

**Solutions:**
- **Immediate:** Provide Linda with manager coaching (executive coach or internal mentor)
- **Short-term:** Enroll Linda in manager training program
- **Long-term:** Create manager readiness criteria + require training before promotion to management

---

### Ishikawa (Fishbone) Diagram

**Alternative root cause method (visual):**

**Problem:** Workload/burnout scored 60% favorable

**Categories (fishbone):**
- **People:** Headcount hasn't kept up with growth
- **Process:** No process to say "no" to new projects (everything is top priority)
- **Tools:** Inefficient tools slow down work
- **Culture:** "Always-on" culture (emails at 10pm, weekend Slack messages)
- **Leadership:** Executives keep adding initiatives without removing old ones

**Root causes identified:**
1. Headcount gap (People)
2. Lack of prioritization (Process)
3. Culture of overwork (Culture)

**Solutions:**
- Accelerate hiring (People)
- Implement project prioritization framework (Process)
- Leadership models work-life balance (Culture)

## Step 3: Define SMART Actions

**Every action must be:**
- **S**pecific
- **M**easurable
- **A**ssignable (who owns it)
- **R**ealistic
- **T**ime-bound

---

### Template: SMART Action Plan

**Goal:** Improve [metric] from [current] to [target] by [date]

**Actions:**

| # | Action | Owner | Deadline | Success Metric |
|---|--------|-------|----------|----------------|
| 1 | [Specific action] | [Name] | [Date] | [Measurable outcome] |

---

**Goal:** Improve "I have opportunities for career growth" from 45% to 65% favorable within 12 months

| 5 | Publish internal mobility policy (make it easy to switch teams) | VP of People | Q2 2025 | Policy published on internal wiki |
| 6 | Measure progress in Q3 pulse survey | VP of People | Q3 2025 | Career development score improves to 60%+ (interim goal) |

---

### Example 2: Workload/Burnout Action Plan

**Goal:** Improve "My workload is reasonable and manageable" from 60% to 75% favorable within 9 months

## Step 4: Assign Ownership

**Every action needs a directly responsible individual (DRI).**

---

### Ownership Roles

**1. Executive Sponsor**
- **Role:** Remove roadblocks, allocate budget, hold DRI accountable
- **Example:** CEO sponsors "Career Development" action plan (signals importance)

**2. Directly Responsible Individual (DRI)**
- **Role:** Does the work, drives execution, reports progress
- **Example:** VP of People is DRI for creating career ladders

**3. Contributors**
- **Role:** Support the DRI (subject matter experts, cross-functional partners)
- **Example:** Department heads help define role-specific career competencies

---

### Example: Ownership Chart

**Action:** Create career ladders for all roles

| Role | Name | Responsibilities |
|------|------|------------------|
| **Executive Sponsor** | CEO | Approves framework, allocates $50K budget, quarterly check-ins |
| **DRI** | VP of People | Leads design, coordinates with dept heads, delivers final career ladders |
| **Contributors** | VP Eng, VP Sales, VP Ops | Define competencies for their departments |
| **Contributors** | L&D Manager | Designs training to support career paths |

---

### RACI Matrix (Optional)

**For complex projects, use RACI:**
- **R**esponsible: Does the work (DRI)
- **A**ccountable: Final decision-maker (Executive Sponsor)
- **C**onsulted: Provides input (Contributors)
- **I**nformed: Kept in the loop (stakeholders)

**Example: Career Ladders Project**

| Task | VP People | CEO | Dept Heads | L&D | Employees |
|------|-----------|-----|------------|-----|-----------|
| Define framework | R | A | C | C | I |
| Get dept input | R | I | C | I | I |
| Finalize ladders | R | A | I | I | I |
| Communicate ladders | R | I | I | R | I |
| Train managers | C | I | I | R | I |

## Step 6: Implementation Playbook

**How to actually execute the action plan:**

---

### Week 1-2: Finalize Action Plan

**Tasks:**
- [ ] Review survey results with leadership
- [ ] Prioritize top 3-5 issues
- [ ] Draft SMART actions
- [ ] Assign owners (get commitments)
- [ ] Get CEO approval and sponsorship

**Deliverable:** Final action plan document

---

### Week 3: Communicate Results + Action Plan

**Tasks:**
- [ ] CEO sends company-wide email with survey results + action plan
- [ ] Host all-hands to present results (transparency)
- [ ] Managers receive team-level reports + guide for discussing with teams
- [ ] Post action plan on internal wiki (visible to all)

**Template:** See `survey-communication-templates.md`

---

### Month 1-3: Execute Quick Wins

**Why quick wins?** Build momentum and trust ("they're actually doing something!")

**Examples of quick wins:**
- ‚úÖ Launch "No Meeting Fridays" (immediate relief)
- ‚úÖ Cancel 3 low-value projects (immediate workload reduction)
- ‚úÖ Publish internal mobility policy (shows commitment to career growth)
- ‚úÖ CEO commits to no emails after 6pm (leadership models balance)

**Avoid:** Long-term initiatives with no visible progress for 6+ months (employees lose faith)

---

### Ongoing: Track Progress

**Monthly check-ins:**
- [ ] Action owners report progress (green/yellow/red status)
- [ ] Escalate blockers to executive sponsors
- [ ] Update company on progress (monthly email or all-hands update)

## Step 7: Avoid Common Pitfalls

---

### Pitfall 1: Boiling the ocean

**Mistake:** Try to fix every survey issue (20+ action items)

**Why it fails:** Diluted resources, no clear ownership, nothing gets done

**Fix:** Pick 3-5 top priorities, execute them well

---

### Pitfall 2: Vague actions

**Mistake:**
- ‚ùå "Improve career development"
- ‚ùå "Better communication"
- ‚ùå "Increase engagement"

**Why it fails:** No one knows what to do, no accountability

**Fix:** SMART actions
- ‚úÖ "Create career ladders for all roles by Q2 (Owner: VP People)"
- ‚úÖ "Train 25 managers on career conversations by Q1 (Owner: L&D)"

---

### Pitfall 3: No ownership

**Mistake:** "HR will handle survey action items"

**Why it fails:** HR can't fix workload, compensation, or manager quality alone (these are business issues, not HR issues)

**Fix:** Assign business owners
- Career development ‚Üí VP of People + Department Heads
- Workload ‚Üí VP of Operations + Hiring
- Manager quality ‚Üí Managers' managers (coaching)

---

### Pitfall 4: Action plan sits in drawer

**Mistake:** Create action plan, then forget about it

**Why it fails:** No progress, employees lose trust

**Fix:**
- Monthly progress updates (dashboard, all-hands)
- Quarterly pulse survey to measure impact
- Tie action plan to executive goals/OKRs (make it a priority)

---

### Pitfall 5: No quick wins

**Mistake:** All actions are 12-month initiatives (e.g., "Build new career framework")

**Why it fails:** Employees don't see progress, lose faith

**Fix:** Mix of quick wins (1-3 months) and long-term initiatives
- **Quick wins:** No Meeting Fridays, cancel low-value projects, CEO commits to work-life balance
- **Long-term:** Career ladders, comp bands, manager training program

## Step 8: Measuring ROI of Actions

**How do you know if your actions worked?**

---

### Method 1: Survey Score Improvement

**Before/after comparison:**

| Metric | Before Action | After Action (12 months) | Change |
|--------|---------------|--------------------------|--------|
| Career development | 45% favorable | 68% favorable | +23 points ‚úÖ |
| eNPS | +25 | +38 | +13 points ‚úÖ |

**Insight:** Career development actions worked (23-point improvement)

---

### Method 2: Business Impact

**Example: Manager Training Program**

**Investment:**
- Manager training: $50K (external program for 25 managers)
- Time cost: 25 managers √ó 40 hours = 1,000 hours (~$50K in salary)
- **Total cost: $100K**

**Impact (12 months later):**
- Manager effectiveness score: 75% ‚Üí 85% (+10 points)
- Turnover (manager-related): 15% ‚Üí 8% (-7 points)
- Employees retained: 17 (7% of 250 employees)

**Financial benefit:**
- Cost to replace 1 employee: $30K (recruiting + onboarding + lost productivity)
- Benefit: 17 employees √ó $30K = $510K

**ROI:**
```
ROI = ($510K - $100K) / $100K √ó 100% = 410%

For every $1 spent on manager training, company saved $5.10
```

---

### Method 3: Retention Analysis

**Track whether survey respondents stay or leave:**

**Example:**

| Group | 12-Month Retention Rate |
|-------|-------------------------|
| Promoters (eNPS 9-10) | 95% ‚úÖ |
| Passives (eNPS 7-8) | 85% üü° |
| Detractors (eNPS 0-6) | 60% ‚ùå |

**Insight:** Detractors are 35 points more likely to leave (95% - 60%)

**Action:** If you improve engagement (convert Detractors ‚Üí Passives/Promoters), you reduce turnover

**Financial impact:**
- 40 Detractors (20% of 200 employees)
- If 40% of Detractors leave: 16 employees quit
- Cost to replace 16 employees: 16 √ó $30K = $480K

**If action plan converts half of Detractors ‚Üí Passives:**
- Detractors: 40 ‚Üí 20
- Turnover: 16 ‚Üí 8 employees quit
- **Savings: 8 √ó $30K = $240K**

## Key Takeaways

1. **Prioritize ruthlessly** - Pick 3-5 top issues, not 20
2. **Find root causes** - Don't treat symptoms
3. **SMART actions** - Specific, Measurable, Assignable, Realistic, Time-bound
4. **Assign ownership** - Every action needs a DRI
5. **Mix quick wins + long-term** - Quick wins build trust
6. **Track progress** - Monthly updates, quarterly pulse survey
7. **Measure ROI** - Prove impact (survey scores + business metrics)
8. **Communicate relentlessly** - Keep employees informed on progress

**Great action plans = clear priorities, strong ownership, visible progress.**
