### Overview: Communication Principles

**The golden rule:** If you ask for feedback, you must close the loop.

**Communication flow:**
```
Pre-Survey (Build Excitement) ‚Üí During Survey (Drive Participation) ‚Üí Results Announcement (Transparency) ‚Üí Action Plan (Commitment) ‚Üí Progress Updates (Accountability) ‚Üí Next Survey (Show Impact)
```

**Why communication matters:**
- ‚úÖ **Good communication:** Builds trust, drives engagement, increases next year's response rate
- ‚ùå **Bad communication:** "We asked, nothing changed" ‚Üí Survey response rate tanks ‚Üí You lose valuable feedback channel

### Communication Best Practices

#### 1. Transparency

**Do:**
- ‚úÖ Share results company-wide (overall + department-level)
- ‚úÖ Acknowledge bad news ("Career development scored low. We need to fix this.")
- ‚úÖ Show data (charts, heat maps, trends)

**Don't:**
- ‚ùå Hide results ("Survey results are confidential, leadership only")
- ‚ùå Spin bad news ("Our engagement is only 50%, but that's great for our industry!") ‚Üê dishonest
- ‚ùå Cherry-pick data ("Look at our high scores, ignore the low ones")

---

#### 2. Speed

**Timeline:**
- Survey closes ‚Üí Results shared within 3-4 weeks (no more than 1 month)
- Why? Employees lose interest if you wait too long

**Bad example:**
- Survey closes in November ‚Üí "We'll share results in Q1" (3+ months later) ‚ùå

**Good example:**
- Survey closes Nov 15 ‚Üí Results shared at Dec 10 all-hands (3.5 weeks) ‚úÖ

---

#### 3. Action-Oriented

**Every communication must include:**
1. **What we heard** (survey results)
2. **What we're doing** (action plan)
3. **How we'll track progress** (milestones, timeline)

**Bad example:**
- "Thank you for completing the survey. We'll review and get back to you." ‚ùå (vague, no action)

**Good example:**
- "Career development scored 45%. We're creating career ladders (by Q2), training managers (by Q1), and launching Lunch & Learn (by Q2). We'll measure progress in Q3 pulse survey." ‚úÖ (specific, actionable)

## Part 2: Action Planning Framework

Complete guide to translating survey insights into concrete action plans that drive organizational change.

---

### Overview: From Insights to Action

**The bridge:**
```
Survey Results ‚Üí Prioritization ‚Üí Root Cause Analysis ‚Üí SMART Actions ‚Üí Ownership ‚Üí Implementation ‚Üí Measurement
```

**Common failure mode:**
- ‚ùå Share survey results ‚Üí "Thanks for feedback!" ‚Üí Nothing changes ‚Üí Next year's survey response rate tanks

**Success model:**
- ‚úÖ Share results ‚Üí Prioritized action plan ‚Üí Clear owners ‚Üí Execute ‚Üí Measure impact ‚Üí Communicate progress

---

### Step 1: Prioritization

**You can't fix everything. Prioritize ruthlessly.**

---

#### The 2x2 Prioritization Matrix

**Axes:**
- **X-axis:** Impact on engagement (Low ‚Üí High)
- **Y-axis:** Current score (High ‚Üí Low)

**Quadrants:**

|                     | Low Impact | High Impact |
|---------------------|------------|-------------|
| **Low Score (Problem)** | üü° Nice to have | üî¥ **PRIORITY 1** |
| **High Score (Strength)** | ‚ö™ Maintain | üü¢ Protect |

---

**üî¥ Priority 1: High Impact + Low Score**
- **Action:** Fix these first (biggest ROI)
- **Example:** Career development (45% favorable, 0.75 correlation to eNPS)

**üü¢ Protect: High Impact + High Score**
- **Action:** Don't break what's working (keep doing it)
- **Example:** Manager effectiveness (85% favorable, 0.72 correlation to eNPS)

**üü° Nice to have: Low Impact + Low Score**
- **Action:** Deprioritize (won't move engagement)
- **Example:** Office comfort (50% favorable, 0.20 correlation to eNPS)

**‚ö™ Maintain: Low Impact + High Score**
- **Action:** No change needed
- **Example:** Free snacks (80% favorable, 0.10 correlation to eNPS)

---

#### The 3-5 Rule

**Pick 3-5 top priorities. No more.**

**Why?**
- **Trying to fix 10 things = fixing nothing** (diluted resources, no accountability)
- **Focus on 3-5 = real progress** (concentrated effort, clear ownership)

**Exception:** If you have segmented issues (e.g., Operations department crisis), create department-specific action plan.

---

### Step 2: Root Cause Analysis

**Don't treat symptoms. Find root causes.**

---

#### The 5 Whys Technique

**Example 1: Career Development**

**Problem:** "I have opportunities for career growth" = 45% favorable

**5 Whys:**
1. **Why is career growth scored low?**
   - "Employees don't see promotion opportunities"

2. **Why don't they see promotion opportunities?**
   - "No clear criteria for promotion (what does it take to get promoted?)"

3. **Why don't we have clear promotion criteria?**
   - "We never documented career ladders"

4. **Why haven't we documented career ladders?**
   - "Company grew fast (30 ‚Üí 250 employees in 2 years), systems didn't keep up"

5. **Why didn't systems keep up?**
   - "HR is under-resourced (1 HRBP for 250 employees)"

**Root cause:** HR capacity issue + lack of documented career frameworks

**Solutions:**
- **Short-term:** Train managers to have career conversations with their teams (even without formal ladders)
- **Medium-term:** Hire HR to build career ladders (or contract with external consultant)
- **Long-term:** Build career development into performance review process

---

### Step 3: Define SMART Actions

**Every action must be:**
- **S**pecific
- **M**easurable
- **A**ssignable (who owns it)
- **R**ealistic
- **T**ime-bound

---

**Actions:**

### Step 4: Assign Ownership

**Every action needs a directly responsible individual (DRI).**

---

#### Ownership Roles

**1. Executive Sponsor**
- **Role:** Remove roadblocks, allocate budget, hold DRI accountable
- **Example:** CEO sponsors "Career Development" action plan (signals importance)

**2. Directly Responsible Individual (DRI)**
- **Role:** Does the work, drives execution, reports progress
- **Example:** VP of People is DRI for creating career ladders

**3. Contributors**
- **Role:** Support the DRI (subject matter experts, cross-functional partners)
- **Example:** Department heads help define role-specific career competencies

### Step 6: Avoid Common Pitfalls

---

#### Pitfall 1: Boiling the ocean

**Mistake:** Try to fix every survey issue (20+ action items)

**Why it fails:** Diluted resources, no clear ownership, nothing gets done

**Fix:** Pick 3-5 top priorities, execute them well

---

#### Pitfall 2: Vague actions

**Mistake:**
- ‚ùå "Improve career development"
- ‚ùå "Better communication"
- ‚ùå "Increase engagement"

**Why it fails:** No one knows what to do, no accountability

**Fix:** SMART actions
- ‚úÖ "Create career ladders for all roles by Q2 (Owner: VP People)"
- ‚úÖ "Train 25 managers on career conversations by Q1 (Owner: L&D)"

---

#### Pitfall 3: No ownership

**Mistake:** "HR will handle survey action items"

**Why it fails:** HR can't fix workload, compensation, or manager quality alone (these are business issues, not HR issues)

**Fix:** Assign business owners
- Career development ‚Üí VP of People + Department Heads
- Workload ‚Üí VP of Operations + Hiring
- Manager quality ‚Üí Managers' managers (coaching)

---

#### Pitfall 4: Action plan sits in drawer

**Mistake:** Create action plan, then forget about it

**Why it fails:** No progress, employees lose trust

**Fix:**
- Monthly progress updates (dashboard, all-hands)
- Quarterly pulse survey to measure impact
- Tie action plan to executive goals/OKRs (make it a priority)

---

#### Pitfall 5: No quick wins

**Mistake:** All actions are 12-month initiatives (e.g., "Build new career framework")

**Why it fails:** Employees don't see progress, lose faith

**Fix:** Mix of quick wins (1-3 months) and long-term initiatives
- **Quick wins:** No Meeting Fridays, cancel low-value projects, CEO commits to work-life balance
- **Long-term:** Career ladders, comp bands, manager training program

---

## Key Takeaways

**Communication:**
1. **Close the loop** - If you ask for feedback, you must communicate what you're doing with it
2. **Be transparent** - Share good and bad news (hiding results destroys trust)
3. **Be fast** - Communicate results within 3-4 weeks of survey close
4. **Pair results with action** - Never share results without action plan
5. **Show progress** - Monthly updates, quarterly pulse surveys
6. **Be honest** - Don't over-promise, acknowledge challenges
7. **Create dialogue** - Two-way communication (Q&A, office hours, Slack)

**Action Planning:**
1. **Prioritize ruthlessly** - Pick 3-5 top issues, not 20
2. **Find root causes** - Don't treat symptoms
3. **SMART actions** - Specific, Measurable, Assignable, Realistic, Time-bound
4. **Assign ownership** - Every action needs a DRI
5. **Mix quick wins + long-term** - Quick wins build trust
6. **Track progress** - Monthly updates, quarterly pulse survey
7. **Measure ROI** - Prove impact (survey scores + business metrics)
8. **Communicate relentlessly** - Keep employees informed on progress

**Great communication = transparency + speed + action + consistency.**
**Great action plans = clear priorities, strong ownership, visible progress.**
