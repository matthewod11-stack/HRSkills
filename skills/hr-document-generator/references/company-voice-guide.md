# Company Voice Guide

## Tone and Style

### Overall Voice
- **Professional but warm** - We're serious about our work, but we're also human
- **Clear and direct** - No jargon or corporate-speak
- **Respectful and inclusive** - Every employee deserves dignity

### Writing Principles

1. **Use active voice**
   - ✅ "We are pleased to offer you..."
   - ❌ "It has been decided that you will be offered..."

2. **Be specific**
   - ✅ "Your base salary will be $120,000 per year"
   - ❌ "You will receive competitive compensation"

3. **Write for clarity**
   - ✅ "Your first day will be Monday, January 15, 2025"
   - ❌ "Commencement of employment shall occur on the third Monday of the inaugural month"

4. **Be human**
   - ✅ "We're excited to have you join the team!"
   - ❌ "The organization acknowledges your pending integration"

### Company Values (to reflect in documents)

- **Excellence** - We set high standards
- **Collaboration** - We succeed together
- **Innovation** - We embrace new ideas
- **Integrity** - We do the right thing
- **Inclusivity** - Everyone belongs here

### Document-Specific Guidelines

#### Offer Letters
- **Tone:** Enthusiastic and welcoming
- **Focus:** Make the candidate excited to join
- **Include:** Clear details, warm welcome, next steps

#### Performance Improvement Plans
- **Tone:** Direct but supportive
- **Focus:** Path to improvement, not punishment
- **Include:** Specific issues, clear expectations, resources

#### Termination Letters
- **Tone:** Professional and respectful
- **Focus:** Facts, logistics, dignity
- **Include:** Clear information, no unnecessary explanation

#### Reference Letters
- **Tone:** Positive and professional
- **Focus:** Factual accomplishments
- **Include:** Specific examples, measurable results

## Words to Use / Avoid

### Use:
- Team, collaborate, partner
- Grow, develop, learn
- Support, enable, empower
- Clear, transparent, direct
- Respect, dignity, fairness

### Avoid:
- Synergy, leverage, utilize
- Paradigm shift, circle back
- Rock star, ninja, guru
- Family (we're a team, not a family)
- Overly formal legal language (unless legally required)

## Examples

### Good Example (Offer Letter Opening)
"We're excited to offer you the position of Senior Engineer on our Platform team. Your experience building scalable systems and your collaborative approach make you a great fit for what we're building."

### Bad Example
"Pursuant to our previous communications and subsequent deliberations, the organization hereby extends an invitation for you to commence employment in the capacity of Senior Engineer within the Platform engineering subdivision."

---

**Remember:** Our voice should make people feel valued and respected while maintaining professionalism.
