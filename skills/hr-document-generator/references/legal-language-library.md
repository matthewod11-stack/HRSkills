# Legal Language Library

State-specific and federally required clauses for employment documents.

**⚠️ Important:** This is a template library. Always have employment counsel review final documents.

## At-Will Employment

### Standard Clause (Most States)
```
This letter constitutes an offer of at-will employment. This means that either you or [Company Name] may terminate the employment relationship at any time, with or without cause or notice. This at-will relationship cannot be modified except in a written agreement signed by you and an authorized representative of [Company Name].
```

### Montana Exception
Montana is NOT an at-will state after probationary period. Use:
```
This offer is for employment during a probationary period of [typically 6-12 months]. During this time, employment may be terminated by either party with or without cause. Following successful completion of the probationary period, termination must be for good cause as defined by Montana law.
```

## Equal Employment Opportunity

### Federal EEO Statement (All States)
```
[Company Name] is an equal opportunity employer. We do not discriminate on the basis of race, color, religion, sex (including pregnancy, sexual orientation, or gender identity), national origin, age (40 or older), disability, genetic information, or any other legally protected characteristic. All employment decisions are based on qualifications, merit, and business needs.
```

## Background Checks

### FCRA-Compliant Disclosure
```
This offer is contingent upon successful completion of a background check. Before obtaining a background check, [Company Name] will provide you with a separate disclosure and obtain your written authorization. You have the right to receive a copy of the background check report and dispute any inaccurate information.
```

## Immigration Law Compliance

### I-9 Requirement (Federal - All States)
```
This offer is contingent upon your providing documentation of your identity and authorization to work in the United States within three business days of your start date, as required by federal law.
```

## State-Specific Requirements

### California

**Pay Transparency**
```
The salary range for this position is $[MIN] to $[MAX] annually. This range represents the company's good faith estimate of the compensation for this role at the time of posting.
```

**Meal and Rest Break Rights**
```
As a California employee, you are entitled to meal and rest breaks as required by California law. You will receive a detailed explanation of these rights in your onboarding materials.
```

**Harassment Prevention**
```
[Company Name] is committed to maintaining a workplace free from harassment and discrimination. California law requires us to provide harassment prevention training, which you will complete during onboarding.
```

### New York

**Wage Theft Prevention**
```
In accordance with New York Labor Law Section 195, you will receive a written notice containing: your rate of pay, regular payday, your employer's name and contact information, and other required information on or before your first day of work.
```

**Sexual Harassment Prevention**
```
New York State law requires all employers to adopt a sexual harassment prevention policy and provide annual training. You will receive our policy and complete training during onboarding.
```

### Colorado

**Pay Transparency (Required in Job Postings)**
```
Compensation: $[MIN] - $[MAX] annually
Benefits: [Must list general benefits]
Application Deadline: [If any]
```

**Equal Pay for Equal Work Act Notice**
```
[Company Name] complies with the Colorado Equal Pay for Equal Work Act. Promotional opportunities and job openings are posted internally and made available to current employees.
```

### Massachusetts

**Parental Leave**
```
Massachusetts law provides eligible employees with up to 12 weeks of paid family and medical leave. You will receive detailed information about this benefit during onboarding.
```

**Non-Compete Restrictions**
```
[If applicable] Massachusetts law places restrictions on non-compete agreements. Any non-compete agreement must: (1) be supported by a garden leave clause or other mutually agreed upon consideration, (2) be no broader than necessary to protect legitimate business interests, (3) not last longer than 12 months from termination, and (4) be reasonable in geographic scope.
```

### Texas

**Right to Work**
```
Texas is a right-to-work state. Membership in a labor organization is not required as a condition of employment.
```

### Washington

**Paid Sick Leave**
```
Washington law provides all employees with paid sick leave. You will accrue one hour of paid sick leave for every 40 hours worked. Details are provided in your onboarding materials.
```

## Benefits Continuation (COBRA)

### Federal Requirement
```
If your employment ends, you may be eligible to continue your health insurance coverage under COBRA (Consolidated Omnibus Budget Reconciliation Act). You will receive detailed information about COBRA rights at the time of termination.
```

## Arbitration Agreements

### Optional Clause (Check State Law)
⚠️ Some states restrict mandatory arbitration. Review carefully.

```
Any disputes arising out of your employment will be resolved through binding arbitration rather than in court, except where prohibited by law. You will receive a separate arbitration agreement for your review and signature.
```

**States with Restrictions:**
- California: Cannot require arbitration of harassment/discrimination claims
- New York: Restricts mandatory arbitration for harassment
- New Jersey: Limits pre-dispute arbitration agreements

## Non-Solicitation Agreements

### General Template
```
During your employment and for [6-12 months] after termination, you agree not to solicit [Company Name] employees to leave their employment or solicit company customers or clients for a competing business.
```

**Note:** Enforceability varies by state. Some states (e.g., California) heavily restrict non-solicitation agreements.

## Severance Agreements

### Release Template
```
In exchange for the severance benefits described above, you agree to release [Company Name] from any and all claims arising out of your employment or its termination. This release includes, but is not limited to, claims under Title VII, ADA, ADEA, and state employment laws.

You have [21/45 days] to consider this agreement and 7 days after signing to revoke it. This agreement is not effective until the revocation period has expired.
```

**Requirements:**
- 21 days to consider (standard terminations)
- 45 days to consider (group terminations under ADEA)
- 7 days to revoke after signing
- Must advise to consult with attorney

## Stock Options and Equity

### Standard Clause
```
You will be eligible to participate in the company's equity incentive plan, subject to approval by the Board of Directors. Any equity grants will be governed by the plan documents and your individual grant agreement.
```

**Tax Advisory Language:**
```
Stock options may have tax consequences. We strongly recommend you consult with a tax advisor regarding the tax implications of your equity compensation.
```
