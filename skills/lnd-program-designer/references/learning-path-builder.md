# Learning Path Builder

Complete guide to building progressive learning journeys that develop employees from beginner to expert.

---

## What is a Learning Path?

**Learning path = Structured journey that takes learner from Point A (beginner) to Point B (expert) through progressive skill development.**

**Why learning paths matter:**
- **Clear progression:** Employees know what to learn next
- **Motivation:** Visible progress toward mastery
- **Retention:** Structured development keeps people engaged
- **Business alignment:** Paths tied to roles = right skills at right time

---

## Learning Path Structure

### 3-Level Framework

**Level 1: Foundation (Beginner)**
- **Goal:** Functional in role
- **Duration:** 0-6 months
- **Focus:** Core knowledge and basic skills
- **Assessment:** Can perform day-to-day tasks with supervision

**Level 2: Proficient (Intermediate)**
- **Goal:** Independent contributor
- **Duration:** 6-24 months
- **Focus:** Advanced techniques, complex projects
- **Assessment:** Can own projects end-to-end without supervision

**Level 3: Expert (Advanced)**
- **Goal:** Leader/specialist
- **Duration:** 2+ years
- **Focus:** Leadership, mentorship, innovation
- **Assessment:** Leads projects, mentors others, shapes team direction

## Example 1: Engineering Learning Path

### Level 1: Junior Engineer (0-6 months)

**Goal:** Contribute productively to small projects with mentorship

**Prerequisites:**
- Computer science degree or bootcamp
- Basic programming in [language]
- Git fundamentals

---

**Core Curriculum:**

**Module 1: Onboarding & Codebase Orientation** (Week 1-2)
- Company tech stack overview
- Codebase architecture tour
- Development environment setup
- Code review process
- Shadowing senior engineer (2 days)

**Format:** In-person workshop (2 days) + shadowing

**Assessment:** Complete environment setup, submit first PR (small bug fix)

---

**Module 2: Engineering Fundamentals** (Month 1-2)
- Writing clean, maintainable code
- Testing (unit tests, integration tests)
- Debugging techniques
- Version control best practices
- Security basics

**Format:** Self-paced eLearning (8 hours) + pair programming sessions (4 hours)

**Assessment:** Complete 3 small projects with passing code review

---

**Module 3: Code Review Mastery** (Month 2-3)
- What to look for in code review
- Giving constructive feedback
- Best practices for reviewers and authors

**Format:** Workshop (2 hours) + practice (review 10 PRs with mentor)

**Assessment:** Conduct 5 code reviews independently, reviewed by senior engineer

---

**Module 4: Technical Communication** (Month 3-6)
- Writing technical documentation
- Presenting in stand-ups and demos
- Asking effective questions

**Format:** Workshop (2 hours) + practice

**Assessment:** Write documentation for 1 feature, present 1 demo to team

---

**On-the-Job Application:**
- Complete 5 "good first issues" (small bug fixes)
- Contribute to 1 medium-sized feature (with senior engineer oversight)
- Participate in daily stand-ups and sprint planning

**Level 1 Certification:**
- Complete all modules
- Pass technical assessment (coding challenge)
- Deliver 1 project from design to deployment (with oversight)
- Receive "Meets Expectations" performance rating for 2 consecutive quarters

**Promotion to Level 2:** Mid-Level Engineer

---

### Level 2: Mid-Level Engineer (6-24 months)

**Goal:** Own medium-to-large projects end-to-end, mentor junior engineers

**Prerequisites:** Completed Level 1 certification

---

**Core Curriculum:**

**Module 1: System Design Fundamentals** (Month 6-9)
- Designing scalable systems
- Database design
- API design
- Trade-offs (consistency vs. availability, latency vs. throughput)

**Format:** Self-paced course (12 hours) + design reviews with senior engineers

**Assessment:** Design and document 1 system (reviewed by tech lead)

---

**Module 2: Performance & Optimization** (Month 9-12)
- Profiling and debugging performance issues
- Database query optimization
- Caching strategies
- Load testing

**Format:** Workshop (4 hours) + hands-on project

**Assessment:** Optimize existing feature to improve performance by 30%+

---

**Module 3: Technical Leadership** (Month 12-18)
- Leading technical discussions
- Mentoring junior engineers
- Influencing without authority

**Format:** Workshop (4 hours) + coaching sessions

**Assessment:** Mentor 1 junior engineer for 6 months, lead 1 technical design review

---

**Module 4: Advanced Topics** (Month 18-24) [Choose 1-2 specializations]
- Distributed systems
- Machine learning fundamentals
- Frontend architecture
- DevOps/Infrastructure

**Format:** Self-paced courses + certification (vendor certs or internal)

**Assessment:** Apply specialization to 1 major project

---

**On-the-Job Application:**
- Own 2-3 medium projects end-to-end (from design to deployment)
- Conduct 10+ code reviews per month
- Mentor 1 junior engineer
- Lead 1 on-call rotation

**Level 2 Certification:**
- Complete all modules
- Deliver 2 major projects independently
- Mentor 1 junior engineer to successful project completion
- Receive "Exceeds Expectations" performance rating for 2 consecutive quarters

**Promotion to Level 3:** Senior Engineer

---

### Level 3: Senior Engineer (2+ years)

**Goal:** Technical leader, architect, influence across org

**Prerequisites:** Completed Level 2 certification

---

**Core Curriculum:**

**Module 1: Software Architecture** (Ongoing)
- Microservices vs. monolith
- Event-driven architecture
- Design patterns at scale
- System design interviews (to teach others)

**Format:** Self-study + architecture reviews with Principal Engineers

**Assessment:** Design architecture for 1 major system, present to eng leadership

---

**Module 2: Engineering Leadership** (Ongoing)
- Technical strategy and roadmapping
- Cross-functional collaboration (PM, Design, Data)
- Hiring and interviewing
- Building engineering culture

**Format:** Leadership cohort (6-month program with other senior engineers)

**Assessment:** Lead 1 cross-functional initiative, hire 2 engineers, present at engineering all-hands

---

**Module 3: Thought Leadership** (Ongoing)
- Technical writing (blog posts, RFCs)
- Conference speaking
- Open-source contributions
- Teaching/training others

**Format:** Self-driven + speaking/writing coach

**Assessment:** Publish 2 technical blog posts, speak at 1 conference or internal tech talk

---

**On-the-Job Application:**
- Own critical systems and architecture decisions
- Lead 1 major cross-team initiative
- Mentor 2-3 engineers (mix of junior and mid-level)
- Participate in hiring (conduct 10+ interviews/year)
- On-call leadership (incident response, root cause analysis)

**Level 3 Certification:**
- Complete modules (ongoing, no fixed timeline)
- Lead 1 major architectural project
- Mentor team to successful delivery
- Recognized as technical expert (peer feedback, 360 reviews)

**Promotion to Principal Engineer (Staff+):** Organizational impact, technical vision

## Example 2: Sales Learning Path

### Level 1: Sales Development Rep (SDR) (0-6 months)

**Goal:** Generate qualified leads and book meetings for Account Executives

**Prerequisites:** None (entry-level role)

---

**Core Curriculum:**

**Module 1: Sales Foundations** (Week 1)
- Sales process overview
- Product knowledge (what we sell, who we sell to)
- Ideal customer profile (ICP)
- Value proposition

**Format:** In-person bootcamp (1 week)

**Assessment:** Pass product knowledge quiz (90%+)

---

**Module 2: Prospecting & Outreach** (Month 1-2)
- Researching prospects
- Cold email templates
- Cold calling techniques
- LinkedIn outreach
- Handling objections

**Format:** Workshop (2 days) + role-plays + call shadowing (10 calls)

**Assessment:** Book 5 qualified meetings in Month 2

---

**Module 3: CRM & Tools** (Month 1)
- Salesforce/HubSpot (logging activities, managing pipeline)
- Sales engagement tools (Outreach, SalesLoft)
- Email tracking and analytics

**Format:** Hands-on training (4 hours)

**Assessment:** Log 50 activities in CRM with 100% data quality

---

**Module 4: Qualification Framework** (Month 2-3)
- BANT (Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline)
- Discovery questions
- Identifying red flags (unqualified leads)

**Format:** Workshop (2 hours) + practice

**Assessment:** Qualify 10 leads using BANT framework (manager review)

---

**On-the-Job Application:**
- 50 outbound calls per day
- 20 emails per day
- Book 5-10 qualified meetings per month
- Attend all booked meetings (with AE)

**Level 1 Certification:**
- Complete all modules
- Achieve quota: 8 qualified meetings/month for 2 consecutive months
- Pass role-play assessment (objection handling)

**Promotion to Level 2:** Account Executive (AE)

---

### Level 2: Account Executive (6-24 months)

**Goal:** Close deals, manage full sales cycle, hit quota

**Prerequisites:** Completed SDR certification

---

**Core Curriculum:**

**Module 1: Full-Cycle Selling** (Month 6-9)
- Discovery calls (asking great questions)
- Demo best practices
- Proposal writing
- Negotiation techniques
- Closing strategies

**Format:** Workshop (3 days) + shadowing top AE (5 demos)

**Assessment:** Run 3 full sales cycles from discovery to close (manager observes)

---

**Module 2: Deal Strategy** (Month 9-12)
- Account planning (mapping stakeholders, building champions)
- Multi-threading (engaging multiple buyers)
- Competitive positioning
- Managing complex deals

**Format:** Self-paced course (6 hours) + deal review sessions

**Assessment:** Close 1 complex deal (6+ stakeholders)

---

**Module 3: Pipeline Management** (Month 12-18)
- Forecasting accuracy
- Time management (prioritizing high-value opportunities)
- Pipeline velocity (speeding up sales cycle)

**Format:** Workshop (2 hours) + coaching

**Assessment:** Maintain forecast accuracy within 10% for 3 consecutive months

---

**Module 4: Customer Success Handoff** (Month 18-24)
- Setting customer expectations
- Smooth handoff to Customer Success team
- Upsell/cross-sell identification during sales process

**Format:** Workshop (2 hours)

**Assessment:** 90%+ customer satisfaction on post-sale survey

---

**On-the-Job Application:**
- Manage 20-30 active opportunities
- Close $500K-$1M ARR annually (quota)
- Attend industry events/conferences
- Maintain 3x pipeline coverage

**Level 2 Certification:**
- Complete all modules
- Hit quota (100%+) for 2 consecutive quarters
- Customer satisfaction score 90%+
- Pipeline management: 3x coverage consistently

**Promotion to Level 3:** Senior AE or Sales Manager

---

### Level 3: Senior AE / Sales Leader (2+ years)

**Goal:** Close enterprise deals, mentor team, influence sales strategy

**Prerequisites:** Completed AE certification

---

**Core Curriculum:**

**Module 1: Enterprise Sales** (Ongoing)
- Selling to C-level executives
- Complex, multi-year deals
- RFP response strategies
- Legal/procurement navigation

**Format:** Self-study + coaching from VP Sales

**Assessment:** Close 1 enterprise deal ($100K+ ARR)

---

**Module 2: Sales Leadership** (Ongoing)
- Coaching and mentoring SDRs/AEs
- Running effective pipeline reviews
- Hiring and interviewing
- Building sales culture

**Format:** Leadership cohort (6-month program)

**Assessment:** Mentor 2 AEs, participate in 5 sales hires

---

**Module 3: Strategic Selling** (Ongoing)
- Account-based selling (ABM)
- Partnership development
- Thought leadership (speaking at events, writing content)

**Format:** Self-driven + support from marketing

**Assessment:** Land 1 strategic partnership or speak at 1 industry conference

---

**On-the-Job Application:**
- Close $1M+ ARR annually
- Mentor 2-3 AEs/SDRs
- Contribute to sales strategy (provide feedback to leadership)
- Participate in hiring and onboarding

**Level 3 Recognition:**
- Top 10% of quota achievers
- Mentor team to quota attainment
- Recognized as sales expert (President's Club, awards)

**Promotion to Sales Manager:** Lead team of 5-8 AEs

## Example 3: Product Manager Learning Path

### Level 1: Associate Product Manager (0-6 months)

**Goal:** Support product team, own small features

**Prerequisites:** None (entry-level or career switcher)

---

**Core Curriculum:**

**Module 1: PM Foundations** (Month 1-2)
- What is product management?
- Product development lifecycle
- Agile/Scrum basics
- Stakeholder management (Eng, Design, Sales, Marketing)

**Format:** Self-paced course (8 hours) + shadowing senior PM (2 weeks)

**Assessment:** Shadow senior PM on 1 full feature launch

---

**Module 2: User Research & Customer Discovery** (Month 2-3)
- Conducting user interviews
- Creating user personas
- Jobs-to-be-Done framework
- Analyzing customer feedback

**Format:** Workshop (1 day) + conduct 5 user interviews

**Assessment:** Present insights from 5 user interviews to team

---

**Module 3: Roadmapping & Prioritization** (Month 3-4)
- Writing product requirements (PRDs)
- Prioritization frameworks (RICE, Kano)
- Building roadmaps
- Communicating roadmap to stakeholders

**Format:** Workshop (1 day) + practice

**Assessment:** Write 1 PRD, create roadmap for small feature

---

**Module 4: Working with Engineering & Design** (Month 4-6)
- Technical fluency (understanding architecture, APIs)
- Design collaboration (wireframes, prototypes)
- Sprint planning and backlog grooming
- QA and bug triage

**Format:** Shadowing + workshops (4 hours)

**Assessment:** Own 1 small feature from spec to launch

---

**On-the-Job Application:**
- Own 2-3 small features per quarter
- Participate in sprint planning and stand-ups
- Conduct 10 user interviews
- Support senior PMs on larger initiatives

**Level 1 Certification:**
- Complete all modules
- Launch 2 features successfully (on time, positive feedback)
- Pass product knowledge assessment

**Promotion to Level 2:** Product Manager

---

### Level 2: Product Manager (6-24 months)

**Goal:** Own product area, drive roadmap, influence strategy

**Prerequisites:** Completed APM certification

---

**Core Curriculum:**

**Module 1: Product Strategy** (Month 6-12)
- Defining product vision and strategy
- Competitive analysis
- Market sizing and TAM analysis
- OKRs and metrics

**Format:** Self-paced course (10 hours) + strategy workshops

**Assessment:** Define product strategy for your area, present to leadership

---

**Module 2: Data-Driven Product Management** (Month 12-18)
- Product analytics (SQL, dashboards)
- A/B testing and experimentation
- Metrics that matter (leading vs. lagging indicators)
- Making data-informed decisions

**Format:** Workshop (2 days) + hands-on projects

**Assessment:** Run 2 A/B tests, analyze results, make product decision

---

**Module 3: Influence & Stakeholder Management** (Month 18-24)
- Influencing without authority
- Executive communication
- Managing difficult conversations
- Building relationships across functions

**Format:** Coaching + practice

**Assessment:** Lead 1 cross-functional initiative (Eng, Design, Sales, Marketing)

---

**On-the-Job Application:**
- Own 1 product area (define roadmap, prioritize features)
- Launch 1-2 major features per quarter
- Conduct quarterly business reviews (QBRs) with stakeholders
- Mentor 1 APM

**Level 2 Certification:**
- Complete all modules
- Own successful product area (metrics improving, stakeholder satisfaction)
- Mentor 1 APM

**Promotion to Level 3:** Senior PM or Group PM

---

## Key Principles for Building Learning Paths

1. **Make it progressive** - Each level builds on the previous one

2. **Tie to business outcomes** - Skills should align with company needs

3. **Balance theory and practice** - 70% on-the-job application, 30% formal training

4. **Make progression visible** - Employees know where they are and what's next

5. **Certify mastery** - Clear assessments at each level

6. **Customize for your company** - These are templates, adapt to your context

Great learning paths = clear progression, skill development, business alignment, and motivated employees who see a future at your company.
