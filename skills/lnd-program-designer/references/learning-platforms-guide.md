# Learning Platforms Guide

Complete guide to selecting learning management systems (LMS), authoring tools, and building your L&D tech stack.

---

## Overview: L&D Tech Stack

**A modern L&D tech stack typically includes:**

1. **LMS (Learning Management System)** - Hosts and tracks training
2. **Authoring tools** - Create eLearning content
3. **Video platform** - Host training videos
4. **Virtual classroom** - Live instructor-led training
5. **Content library** - Pre-built courses (optional)

---

## Learning Management Systems (LMS)

**What is an LMS?**

A platform that:
- Hosts training content (eLearning, videos, documents)
- Assigns training to employees
- Tracks completion and progress
- Generates reports (who completed what, scores, time spent)
- Issues certifications

**Think of it as: "Netflix for corporate training"**

---

### When Do You Need an LMS?

**You probably DON'T need an LMS if:**
- <50 employees
- Minimal training needs (onboarding only)
- Budget-constrained

**Alternative:** Use free tools (Google Drive for docs, Zoom for live training, Google Forms for quizzes)

---

**You probably DO need an LMS if:**
- 50+ employees
- Regular training needs (onboarding, compliance, skill development)
- Need to track completion (for compliance, performance reviews)
- Want to scale training without hiring more trainers

---

### LMS Selection Criteria

**1. Ease of Use**

**For admins (L&D team):**
- Can I upload content easily?
- Can I assign training to groups/individuals?
- Can I create learning paths?
- Can I generate reports without IT help?

**For learners (employees):**
- Is it intuitive? (or do I need training to use the training platform? ðŸ¤¦)
- Can I access on mobile?
- Can I bookmark and resume where I left off?

**Red flag:** If demo requires 30-min training on how to use the LMS, it's too complex.

---

**2. Content Compatibility**

**What formats does it support?**
- SCORM (standard eLearning format)
- xAPI/Tin Can (newer standard, better tracking)
- Video (MP4, YouTube embeds)
- Documents (PDF, PowerPoint)
- External links (link to other platforms)

**Must-have:** SCORM 1.2 or 2004 support (most eLearning authoring tools export to SCORM)

---

**3. Reporting & Analytics**

**Key reports:**
- **Completion reports:** Who completed what training?
- **Learner transcripts:** Full training history per employee
- **Compliance reports:** Proof of training for audits
- **Engagement analytics:** Time spent, quiz scores, drop-off points

**Nice-to-have:**
- Custom reports (build your own queries)
- Dashboards (visual charts, not just spreadsheets)
- Automated reporting (scheduled emails)

---

**4. Integration with HRIS**

**Does LMS integrate with your HR system?** (Workday, BambooHR, Rippling, ADP, etc.)

**Why integration matters:**
- **Auto-enrollment:** New hires automatically added to LMS
- **Single sign-on (SSO):** Employees log in once, access LMS
- **Data sync:** Keep employee info (name, title, department) up-to-date

**If no integration:** You'll manually add/remove users (pain with high headcount)

---

**5. Pricing Model**

**Common LMS pricing models:**

**Per-user per-month (PUPM):**
- Example: $5-$15 per user per month
- **Pros:** Predictable, scales with company
- **Cons:** Gets expensive with large headcount

**Per-active-user:**
- Only pay for users who log in that month
- **Pros:** Cost savings if not all employees train every month
- **Cons:** Unpredictable monthly bill

**Flat fee:**
- Fixed annual fee (e.g., $10K-$50K/year for unlimited users)
- **Pros:** Predictable, good for large companies
- **Cons:** Expensive upfront, less flexible

**Freemium:**
- Free for basic features, pay for premium (advanced reports, integrations, branding)
- **Pros:** Low barrier to entry
- **Cons:** Limited features on free tier

---

**6. Support & Onboarding**

**What support is included?**
- Email support? (response time?)
- Phone support?
- Dedicated customer success manager (CSM)?
- Onboarding/training to set up LMS?

**Red flag:** "Email support only, 3-5 business day response time" (too slow if you have an urgent issue)

**Best for:** Large enterprises (1,000+ employees)

**Pros:**
- Comprehensive (LMS + talent management + recruiting)

**Cons:**

**Best for:** Mid-market to enterprise (100-5,000 employees)

**Pros:**
- Modern, intuitive UI
- AI-powered recommendations (suggests training to learners)
- Strong integrations (Salesforce, Workday, Slack, Zoom)
- Great mobile app

**Cons:**
- Mid-high price point
- Some features require add-ons

**Price:** $25K-$50K+ annually (custom pricing)

---

#### 3. **TalentLMS**

**Best for:** Small to mid-sized companies (50-500 employees)

**Pros:**
- Affordable ($149/month for 40 users, scales up)
- Easy to use (setup in <1 day)
- White-label option (custom branding)
- Good mobile experience

**Cons:**
- Limited advanced features
- Basic reporting compared to enterprise LMS

**Price:** Starts at $149/month (40 users), up to $499/month (1,000 users)

---

#### 4. **Absorb LMS**

**Best for:** Mid-market (250-2,500 employees)

**Pros:**
- Highly customizable
- Good integration options
- E-commerce features (sell training externally if needed)
- Strong customer support

**Cons:**
- Pricier than TalentLMS
- Some complexity in setup

**Price:** Custom pricing (typically $15K-$40K annually)

---

#### 5. **360Learning**

**Best for:** Companies focused on peer learning and user-generated content

**Pros:**
- Collaborative learning (employees create courses for each other)
- Modern, social media-like interface
- Good for knowledge sharing culture

**Cons:**
- Different approach (not traditional top-down training)
- Requires cultural buy-in (employees must contribute content)

**Price:** Custom pricing

---

#### 6. **Litmos** (by SAP)

**Best for:** Compliance-heavy industries (healthcare, finance, manufacturing)

**Pros:**
- Strong compliance features (automated reminders, audit trails)
- Content library (2,500+ courses included)
- Good mobile app

**Cons:**
- UI feels dated
- Less flexible than Docebo/Absorb

**Price:** Custom pricing (typically $8-$15 PUPM)

---

#### 7. **Open-Source / Free Options**

**Moodle:**
- **Pros:** Free, highly customizable, large community
- **Cons:** Requires technical setup (hosting, maintenance), not as user-friendly
- **Best for:** Budget-conscious orgs with technical resources

**Canvas (Instructure):**
- **Pros:** Originally built for education, strong feature set, free tier available
- **Cons:** Corporate features less developed than purpose-built LMS
- **Best for:** Education or education-adjacent companies

## Authoring Tools (Creating eLearning Content)

**What are authoring tools?**

Software to create interactive eLearning courses (quizzes, scenarios, branching, animations)

**Output:** SCORM package that uploads to LMS

---

### Top Authoring Tools

#### 1. **Articulate 360** (Industry Standard)

**Includes:**
- **Storyline 360:** Build complex, interactive courses (branching scenarios, custom animations)
- **Rise 360:** Build simple, responsive courses quickly (drag-and-drop, templates)
- **Content library:** Stock photos, characters, templates

**Pros:**
- Industry standard (most L&D pros use it)
- Powerful + easy to learn
- Great support and community
- Mobile-responsive output

**Cons:**
- Subscription only (can't own perpetually)
- Pricey for small teams

**Price:** $1,399/user/year (or $125/month)

**Best for:** Professional L&D teams building custom eLearning

---

#### 2. **Adobe Captivate**

**Pros:**
- One-time purchase option (vs. Articulate subscription)
- VR/AR support (cutting-edge)
- Good for software simulations (screen recordings with interactive hotspots)

**Cons:**
- Steeper learning curve than Articulate
- Less intuitive UI

**Price:** $33/month subscription or $1,299 perpetual license

**Best for:** Teams comfortable with Adobe ecosystem, need VR/AR

---

#### 3. **Camtasia** (Screen Recording & Video Editing)

**Pros:**
- Easy screen recording (great for software tutorials)
- Built-in video editing
- Quizzes and interactivity (basic)

**Cons:**
- Less powerful than Storyline for complex interactions
- More video-focused than eLearning-focused

**Price:** $179.88/year (or $299 perpetual license)

**Best for:** Simple tutorials, software how-tos, video-based training

---

#### 4. **iSpring Suite** (PowerPoint Add-In)

**Pros:**
- Works inside PowerPoint (familiar interface)
- Affordable ($770/user/year)
- Converts PowerPoint to SCORM easily

**Cons:**
- Limited compared to Storyline (less interactivity)
- Tied to PowerPoint (not standalone)

**Price:** $770/user/year

**Best for:** Budget-conscious teams, PowerPoint power users

---

#### 5. **Lectora** (by ELB Learning)

**Pros:**
- Powerful (on par with Storyline)
- Section 508 compliant (accessibility for disabilities)
- Good for large enterprises

**Cons:**
- Expensive
- Steeper learning curve

**Price:** $2,199/user/year

**Best for:** Large enterprises, accessibility-critical training

## Video Hosting Platforms

**Where to host training videos?**

**Option 1: LMS (Built-In Video Hosting)**
- **Pros:** Everything in one place
- **Cons:** Storage limits, transcoding can be slow

**Option 2: YouTube (Unlisted)**
- **Pros:** Free, unlimited storage, fast streaming
- **Cons:** No analytics in LMS (views tracked on YouTube, not LMS), relies on Google

**Option 3: Vimeo**
- **Pros:** Professional, customizable player, analytics
- **Cons:** Paid ($20-$75/month depending on storage)

**Option 4: Wistia**
- **Pros:** Best-in-class analytics (engagement heatmaps, drop-off points)
- **Cons:** Expensive ($99-$319/month)

**Recommendation:**
- **Small budget:** YouTube (unlisted)
- **Professional training:** Vimeo
- **Advanced analytics needed:** Wistia

## Virtual Classroom Platforms

**For live instructor-led training (VILT)**

**Zoom:**
- **Pros:** Everyone knows it, reliable, breakout rooms, polling
- **Cons:** Not designed for training (limited LMS integration, no built-in quizzes)
- **Price:** $15.99/host/month

**Microsoft Teams:**
- **Pros:** Integrated with Microsoft 365, good for corporate settings
- **Cons:** Less interactive features than Zoom
- **Price:** Included with Microsoft 365

**Webex Training:**
- **Pros:** Built for training (tests, polls, breakouts, certifications)
- **Cons:** More expensive, less widely used
- **Price:** $78/host/month

**Recommendation:** Zoom for most companies (ease of use + cost)

## Content Libraries (Pre-Built Courses)

**Buy vs. build trade-off:**
- **Build custom:** Expensive and time-consuming, but tailored to your company
- **Buy pre-built:** Cheap and fast, but generic

**Top content library providers:**

**LinkedIn Learning (formerly Lynda.com):**
- 16,000+ courses (business, tech, creative)
- $29.99/user/month or $379.88/user/year
- Good for: General skills (Excel, project management, communication)

**Skillsoft:**
- Huge library (compliance, leadership, IT)
- Custom pricing (enterprise)
- Good for: Large companies, compliance training

**Udemy Business:**
- 5,500+ curated courses (from Udemy marketplace)
- $360/user/year (minimum 5 users)
- Good for: Tech skills, developer training

**Coursera for Business:**
- University-level courses (Stanford, Yale, Google)
- $399/user/year
- Good for: Technical skills, certifications

## LMS Implementation Checklist

**Phase 1: Planning (Month 1)**
- [ ] Define requirements (what do we need LMS to do?)
- [ ] Get stakeholder buy-in (budget approval)
- [ ] Shortlist vendors (3-5 options)
- [ ] Request demos

**Phase 2: Vendor Selection (Month 2)**
- [ ] Conduct vendor demos
- [ ] Check references (talk to 2-3 current customers)
- [ ] Negotiate pricing and contract
- [ ] Sign contract

**Phase 3: Setup (Month 3-4)**
- [ ] Configure LMS (branding, user roles, settings)
- [ ] Integrate with HRIS (SSO, user sync)
- [ ] Upload initial content (courses, videos, documents)
- [ ] Test (admin and learner experience)

**Phase 4: Pilot (Month 4)**
- [ ] Pilot with small group (10-20 employees)
- [ ] Collect feedback
- [ ] Fix issues

**Phase 5: Launch (Month 5)**
- [ ] Train admins and managers
- [ ] Communicate to all employees (launch announcement)
- [ ] Monitor usage and troubleshoot

**Phase 6: Optimize (Month 6+)**
- [ ] Review reports (completion rates, engagement)
- [ ] Gather feedback (survey employees)
- [ ] Iterate (add content, improve experience)

## Key Takeaways

1. **LMS is a tool, not a strategy** - Don't buy an LMS and expect it to magically create training. You still need to build content and drive adoption.

2. **Start small, scale up** - Don't over-invest in enterprise LMS if you're a 50-person startup. Start with TalentLMS or even free tools, upgrade as you grow.

3. **Ease of use > features** - An LMS with 100 features that no one uses is worse than a simple LMS that everyone actually uses.

4. **Content is king** - The LMS is just the delivery mechanism. Focus on creating great training content.

5. **Integration matters** - SSO and HRIS integration will save you hours of manual work. Worth paying for.

6. **Measure ROI** - Track usage, completion rates, and business impact. Justify your L&D tech investment.

**Great L&D tech = enables scale, drives engagement, proves impact. Choose tools that make your job easier, not harder.**
