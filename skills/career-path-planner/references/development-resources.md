## Books

### Engineering - Technical

**System Design:**
- "Designing Data-Intensive Applications" by Martin Kleppmann (essential for IC2→IC3)
- "System Design Interview" by Alex Xu (interview prep + concepts)
- "Building Microservices" by Sam Newman

**Software Craftsmanship:**
- "Clean Code" by Robert Martin (IC1-IC2)
- "The Pragmatic Programmer" by Hunt & Thomas
- "Refactoring" by Martin Fowler

**Architecture:**
- "Software Architecture: The Hard Parts" by Ford et al.
- "Fundamentals of Software Architecture" by Richards & Ford
- "Domain-Driven Design" by Eric Evans (advanced)

### Engineering - Leadership

**IC Leadership:**
- "Staff Engineer: Leadership Beyond the Management Track" by Will Larson (IC3→IC4)
- "The Staff Engineer's Path" by Tanya Reilly
- "An Elegant Puzzle" by Will Larson (systems thinking)

**Management:**
- "The Manager's Path" by Camille Fournier (essential for IC→Manager)
- "Radical Candor" by Kim Scott (feedback culture)
- "High Output Management" by Andy Grove (classic)
- "Multipliers" by Liz Wiseman (leadership philosophy)

### Product Management

**Foundations:**
- "Inspired" by Marty Cagan (essential for all PMs)
- "The Lean Product Playbook" by Dan Olsen
- "Continuous Discovery Habits" by Teresa Torres

**Advanced:**
- "Good Strategy Bad Strategy" by Richard Rumelt (IC4+)
- "Crossing the Chasm" by Geoffrey Moore (market dynamics)

### Sales

**Fundamentals:**
- "The Challenger Sale" by Dixon & Adamson
- "SPIN Selling" by Neil Rackham
- "Fanatical Prospecting" by Jeb Blount (SDR→AE)

## Stretch Projects

### IC2 → IC3 (Engineer)

**System Design:**
- Lead design for a complex feature (auth, payments, real-time)
- Propose and implement performance improvement (10x faster)
- Design and build internal tool/platform

**Cross-Team:**
- RFC for cross-team change (API redesign)
- Coordinate release across 3+ teams
- On-call rotation leadership

**Mentoring:**
- Mentor new hire through onboarding
- Lead "Lunch & Learn" tech talk
- Write tutorial/guide for team

### IC3 → IC4 (Senior → Staff)

**Strategic Impact:**
- Lead migration project (monolith→microservices)
- Design scalability improvements (10x traffic)
- Technical due diligence for acquisition

**Org-Level:**
- Define engineering standards (testing, deployment)
- Lead working group (security, performance)
- Represent eng in product planning

**External:**
- Speak at conference or meetup
- Contribute to open source
- Write technical blog posts

### IC → Manager

**People Leadership:**
- Mentor intern or new grad (manage project + growth)
- Lead hiring for a role (sourcing, interviews, close)
- Run team retros and facilitate discussions
- Manage a critical incident (coordinate response)

**Management Taste:**
- Shadow current manager (1:1s, planning)
- Take "Introduction to Management" course
- Read "The Manager's Path" book
- Get feedback from peers on leadership

## Development Budget

**Typical Company Budget:**
- Individual contributor: $1,000-$2,000/year
- Manager: $2,000-$3,000/year
- Director+: $3,000-$5,000/year

**Can be used for:**
- Books and courses
- Conference tickets and travel
- Coaching/mentorship programs
- Certifications (AWS, Scrum, etc.)

**Check with HR/Manager for your company policy.**

---

## Time Allocation

**Learning During Work:**
- 10% time (1 day every 2 weeks) - negotiate with manager
- Friday afternoons (quiet time)
- Integrate learning into projects (learn by doing)
- Conference days (1-2 per year)

**Personal Time:**
- Side projects (weekends, optional)
- Reading before bed (30 min/day)
- Courses during evenings (2-3 hours/week)

**Important:** Sustainable > heroic. Don't burn out trying to "level up."

## Tracking Progress

**Keep a brag document:**
- Weekly: What did I accomplish?
- Monthly: What did I learn?
- Quarterly: What impact did I have?
- Annually: Review for performance review + promo packet

**Career Development Log:**
- Books read
- Courses completed
- Projects led
- Skills developed
- Feedback received
- Impact created

**Use this for:**
- Performance reviews
- Promotion packets
- Updating resume
- Reflecting on growth
